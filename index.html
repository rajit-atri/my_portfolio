Who am I?

I am Rajit Palit Atri, an Electrical and Electronic Engineering graduate from the American International University–Bangladesh (AIUB). My academic journey and project work are centered around power systems, smart grid technologies, and data-driven engineering applications. I gained hands-on industrial exposure during my internship at Zodiac Power Chittagong Ltd., where I worked on the auxiliary systems and operational processes of a large-scale power plant.

Alongside my academic work, I have been an active contributor to ESAB, engaging in leadership, event coordination, and creative design tasks that strengthened my teamwork and communication skills.

I am now preparing to pursue graduate research in Power Systems, Smart Grid, Renewable Energy, and ML-assisted energy optimization, with the goal of developing intelligent, efficient, and sustainable engineering solutions for the future.

Digital Stopwatch Using 555 Timer

Undergraduate Project

Hardware stopwatch (0–59 s) using 555 timer, cascaded counters and 7-segment displays.

Designed and implemented a digital stopwatch circuit capable of counting from 0 to 59 seconds using a 555 Timer IC in astable mode. The timer generated precise 1 Hz clock pulses, processed through cascaded counter ICs and BCD-to-7 segment decoders.

Key Methods / Tools

  • 555 Timer IC (astable)
  • Cascaded Counter ICs
  • BCD-to-7 Segment Decoder
  • Digital Electronics
  • Circuit Simulation & Prototyping

Highlights

  • Built an accurate, hardware-based stopwatch prototype.

Buck Converter for Power Management in RC Cars

Undergraduate Project

Integrated a buck converter into a Bluetooth RC car to improve efficiency and runtime.

Built a low-cost Bluetooth-controlled RC car and added a buck converter to step down battery voltage for motors and logic. This improved runtime, reduced heat, and stabilized supply rails for consistent speed and responsiveness.

Key Methods / Tools

  • Buck Converter (DC–DC)
  • DC Motors & Driver ICs
  • Power Budgeting & Thermal Checks
  • Chassis Assembly
  • Circuit Simulation & Prototyping

Highlights

  • Efficiently converted higher battery voltage to stable 5 V/3.3 V rails.
Project Report

1-Bit 4-to-1 MUX Using 2-to-1 MUXes

Undergraduate Project

Hierarchical digital design of a 4-to-1 multiplexer using 2-to-1 MUX blocks in Cadence Virtuoso.

Designed a 1-bit, 4-to-1 multiplexer by combining multiple 1-bit, 2-to-1 MUX units, demonstrating how complex routing logic can be constructed from simpler building blocks. Used Cadence Virtuoso for schematic and transient analysis, including DRC and LVS.

Key Methods / Tools

  • Cadence Virtuoso
  • 2-to-1 MUX Design
  • Schematic Entry
  • Transient Analysis
  • DRC & LVS

Highlights

  • Showcased hierarchical digital circuit composition.
Project Report

Physical Layout of a 2-to-1 MUX with DRC/ERC/LVS

Undergraduate Project

Fabrication-ready layout of a 2-to-1 multiplexer using industry EDA tools and full verification.

Developed the physical layout of a 2-to-1 MUX, translating logical design into layout while respecting fabrication constraints. Verification included Design Rule Check (DRC), Electrical Rule Check (ERC), and Layout vs. Schematic (LVS).

Key Methods / Tools

  • Cadence Virtuoso
  • Physical Design Workflow
  • DRC / ERC / LVS
  • Layout Design

Highlights

  • Produced a fabrication-ready, verified layout for a digital building block.
